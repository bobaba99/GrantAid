# Objectives

The training grants from the Fonds de recherche du Qu√©bec aim to help ensure a new generation of students in all research sectors: natural sciences, mathematical sciences and engineering (Nature and technologies), human health (Health), human and social sciences, arts and letters (Society and culture).

More specifically, the doctoral scholarship aims to:
- Stimulating the interest of the next generation of students in research
- Supporting research excellence by providing financial assistance to the best candidates to begin or continue a doctoral research program

# Financing characteristics

## Value and duration of the stock exchange

The annual value of the doctoral scholarship is $25,000.

The maximum amount that can be allocated is $100,000, which corresponds to 12 funding sessions (12 installments).

## Supplement for training outside Quebec

The supplement for training outside Quebec aims to help applicants undertake or continue a higher education program outside Quebec.

This supplement is only for applicants who indicated a training location outside Quebec at the time of application. It is awarded by each FRQ sector to the ten applications that received the highest ranking across all evaluation committees. Applicants must be Canadian citizens or permanent residents and have a valid RAMQ card (see section 3.5). Any application indicating a training location outside Quebec is automatically considered. Joint supervision programs are not eligible.

The maximum annual value of this supplement is $1,500 , or $500 per session.

# Eligibility requirements

In accordance with Article 2.1 of the General Regulations, to be eligible to apply for a doctoral scholarship, the applicant must have:
- Canadian citizenship or Canadian permanent residence, and
- a valid Quebec Health Insurance Board (RAMQ) card on the contest deadline, or proof that a card has been requested from RAMQ and will be valid on the contest deadline, or if she is in the process of renewing her RAMQ card on the contest deadline, proof that a new card has been requested from RAMQ and a copy of her expired card.

If one or both of the above conditions are not met, the applicant remains eligible provided they submit proof of admission to a program of study at a Quebec university or the consent form from their research supervisor (see section 4.3). Furthermore, they must complete their training in Quebec (see section 3.5).

## Eligibility Period

To apply to the regular stream, the applicant must have been enrolled in a doctoral program for a maximum of 14 full-time sessions before May 1, 2026.

# Required documents from the applicant

## RAMQ card, proof of admission or consent form

Applicants who are Canadian citizens or Canadian permanent residents must include the following with their application form:
- a valid Quebec Health Insurance Card (RAMQ) as of the contest deadline, or
- proof that a card has been requested from RAMQ and that it will be valid on the contest deadline, or
- if she is in the process of renewing her RAMQ card on the contest deadline, proof that a new card has been requested from RAMQ and a copy of her expired card.

In accordance with section 3.6 of these rules, applicants who do not hold Canadian citizenship or Canadian permanent residency, or who do not hold one of the above-mentioned documents, remain eligible for this competition but must complete their training at a university in Quebec. They must then submit one of these two documents:
- proof of admission to a program of study at a university in Quebec, or
- a consent form from the person directing the research at a Quebec university, according to the procedure described in section 4.3

## Transcripts

Any missing, incomplete, illegible, unofficial, non-compliant, or outdated transcript will render the application inadmissible.

A copy of all transcripts is required for all university studies undertaken by the applicant, whether completed or not. This includes transcripts obtained through exchange programs or courses that led to transfer credits, if applicable. Transcripts must show all results obtained in all university courses and training programs completed.

For an ongoing program of study, the transcript must:
- to be up-to-date, meaning that it must have been issued no earlier than July of the year the competition opens, and
- submit all results obtained in the last session completed before the competition deadline, including the summer session, if applicable

The elements that make a transcript official may differ from one university to another. However, each transcript must, in order to be considered official by the Fund:
- be issued by the relevant university institution, and
- present the elements indicating that it is an official transcript for this university. This may include the presence of the university's seal or the registrar's signature, or the words "Official Transcript", etc.

A watermark does not render a transcript unofficial, provided the requirements specified above are met. A transcript delivered in a sealed envelope by the Registrar's Office may be opened and its contents scanned. Transcripts available online must be converted to PDF and attached to the application in the appropriate section of the form. If there is any doubt about the authenticity of a transcript, the Fund reserves the right to verify it with the applicant and, if necessary, with the institution concerned.

Other documents to be attached, if applicable, to the file with the transcripts:
- In the case of transcripts obtained outside North America, or when the average is expressed on a value not listed in the Equivalency Table , a document explaining the applicable grading system and/or demonstrating that the cumulative average obtained is at least equivalent to the required threshold must be included. The document attached to the application must clearly explain the grading system used to ensure a proper understanding of the transcripts by the FRQ and the evaluation committee.
- If no transcript is issued, an explanatory document from the institution (registrar's office or equivalent administrative body) detailing the reasons for the missing transcripts will be provided. The Fund reserves the right to verify the validity of the reasons given.
- If no transcript is issued and the applicant is unable to obtain an explanatory document from the university, an explanatory document from the applicant, endorsed by the competent authority of the host institution, justifying the absence of transcripts, will be required. The Fund reserves the right to verify the validity of the reasons given.

## History of part-time or non-registration sessions

Applicants who have been enrolled part-time, or not enrolled at all, in one or more sessions since the start of the training program for which the scholarship is requested, must provide:
- A certificate from the registrar's office indicating the session history or any other official document issued by a competent authority of the university specifying sessions completed part-time or without registration.

## Other documents to be attached to the form

The applicant must also attach the following documents to the appropriate sections of the application form:
- **Recognitions** (1 page maximum): the applicant must, where applicable, describe the scholarships, prizes and distinctions they have obtained, as well as the terms of their award.
- **Integrated presentation of background** (maximum 1 page): Applicants must establish connections between their experiences and interests, on the one hand, and their career choices, education, and academic and professional path, on the other. Although no evaluation criteria are associated with it, the integrated presentation of background can help the evaluation committee better understand, contextualize, and assess the applicant's academic record, background, and contributions. Applicants are not expected to provide sensitive personal information.
- **Relevant Experiences and Achievements** (maximum 4 pages): Applicants must present and describe their relevant experiences and achievements (scientific, professional, social, etc.) in relation to their research project and background. They must also highlight their capacity for engagement and leadership (within and outside academia) and their ability to foster dialogue between science and society.
- **Research project** (maximum 2 pages): the applicant must describe their research project. They must demonstrate the originality of the project and its potential to contribute to the advancement of knowledge, the clarity and coherence of the research problem, the relevance of the conceptual framework and methodology, as well as the feasibility of the project and the realism of the timeline.
- **Bibliography** (maximum 1 page): Applicants must list the complete references of the works used in the description of the proposed research. In accordance with Article 3.2 of the General Rules and Regulations (RGC), hyperlinks to web pages may be included, in the manner of bibliographic references, where relevant.
- **Document for a second doctorate in another discipline** (1 page maximum, if applicable): the applicant who has obtained a first doctoral degree in research in another discipline must demonstrate that the project to which the application relates will be evaluated by an evaluation committee different from the one that would have evaluated the previous project, in accordance with section 3.3.1. This demonstration must be attached to the "Title and research area" section of the form.

## Documents for exceptional measures

Applicants wishing to take advantage of an exception regarding the eligibility period (see section 3.3.3) must provide:
- **Justification for the request for an extension or exemption from the eligibility period** (maximum 1 page): The applicant must explain the request for an exception and, in the case of a request for an extension, provide the dates and duration of the interruptions, as well as the rate of slowdown in activities, if applicable. This document must be attached to the "Pre-eligibility" section of the form and will not be forwarded to the evaluation committee.
- **Supporting documents for the exemption request** (maximum 5 pages): e.g., medical certificate, disability recognition, child's birth certificate, caregiver tax credit application, official document or letter from the employer, etc. These documents allow the Fund to determine the length of the extension to be granted. They must be attached in the "Pre-eligibility" section of the form and will not be forwarded to the evaluation committee.

# Evalulation

## Academic record and achievements (45 points)

- Transcripts
- Recognition (prizes, awards and scholarships received)
- Relevant achievements (scientific, professional, social, etc.) and experiences (related to the research project or career path)
- Capacity for commitment and leadership (in and outside of academia)
- Ability to foster dialogue between science and society

## Research project (55 points)

- Originality of the project and potential contribution to the advancement of knowledge
- Clarity and coherence of the research problem
- Relevance of the conceptual framework and methodology
- Project feasibility and timeline realism

# Entry into force of the rules

These rules apply to the 2026-2027 financial year.
